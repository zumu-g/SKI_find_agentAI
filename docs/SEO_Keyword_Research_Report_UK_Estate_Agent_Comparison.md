### D. General Proptech / Property Service Names

| Brand | Domain | Naming Pattern | Keyword Content |
|-------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Goodlord | goodlord.co | Compound word | None (brand only) |
| Knock | knock.com | Single word | "knock" (door metaphor) |
| Place | place.com | Single word | "place" (generic property) |
| PropertyData | propertydata.co.uk | Keyword compound | "property" + "data" |

### Key Naming Pattern Observations:

1. **Action Verb + Keyword** is the dominant pattern for comparison sites: GetAgent, ChooseMyAgent, CompareAgents. This is the most SEO-friendly approach.

2. **Single evocative English words** are used by premium/funded brands: Nested, Settled, Boomin, Knock, Place. These prioritise brand memorability over SEO.

3. **Invented/abstract names** (Zoopla, Kova) have zero inherent SEO value but unlimited brand potential. They require significant marketing spend to build recognition.

4. **Keyword-rich domains** are becoming less common among well-funded startups. The SmartBranding analysis of 300+ proptech companies found 67.4% use exact brand match .com domains, prioritising brand over keyword.

5. **The .co.uk extension** is standard for UK-focused property brands. However, securing .com is considered important for long-term brand protection.

## 4. GAP ANALYSIS: HEAVILY SEARCHED TERMS NO BRAND OWNS

| Search Term / Concept | Monthly Volume (est.) | Gap Description |
|----------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **"sell my house"** | 12,000+ | No major UK brand directly incorporates "sell" in its name. "SellMyHouse" or similar is unowned as a comparison brand. Huge intent keyword with no brand-level ownership. |
| **"how much is my house worth"** | 30,000+ | Rightmove and Zoopla provide tools for this, but no comparison brand owns the "worth" / "value" concept in its name. This is the #1 entry query for sellers. |
| **"estate agent fees"** | 3,000+ | Strong commercial intent (people comparing costs). No brand has positioned around fee transparency in its name. |
| **"sell my house fast"** | 8,000+ | Fast-sale companies exist (e.g., We Buy Any House) but no comparison/finder brand owns the "fast" concept. |
| **"good estate agent"** | 1,000+ (growing) | Estate Agent Today reports this is a rapidly rising search term in 2026. No brand owns "good" in this context. |
| **"estate agent near me"** | 22,000+ | The "near me" / local concept is massive and growing with voice search. No comparison brand has "local" or "near" in its name. |
| **"find an estate agent"** | 2,500+ | "Find" is a high-intent action verb. No major brand uses it (GetAgent uses "get" instead). |
| **"value my home" / "value my house"** | 6,000+ | First step in the selling journey. No comparison brand owns "value" in its name. |
| **"switch estate agent"** | Growing | An underserved query -- homeowners unhappy with their current agent. No brand owns this concept. |
| **"independent estate agent"** | Growing | Rising search as consumers look beyond chains. No comparison brand targets this specifically. |

### The Biggest Strategic Gaps:

1. **The "sell" keyword** -- The most commercially valuable word for a platform helping people sell their home, and no comparison brand owns it.

2. **The "worth" / "value" concept** -- "How much is my house worth" is one of the highest-volume queries in the entire UK property space (30,000+/mo), and it represents the exact moment a homeowner begins their selling journey. No comparison brand has claimed this territory.

3. **The "local" / "near me" concept** -- With 22,000+ monthly searches for "estate agents near me" and voice search growing rapidly, no comparison brand has positioned itself around local expertise.

4. **Fee transparency** -- "Estate agent fees" is a 3,000+/mo search with strong commercial intent. No brand name signals fee comparison or transparency.

## 5. MARKET CONTEXT & ADDITIONAL DATA POINTS

### Search Behaviour Statistics
- 78% of house hunters start their search online (via portals, Google, or agent websites)
- Only 9% start at high street agents
- 93% of customers won't go past the first page of Google
- 58% of consumers now use voice search to find local businesses
- 55%+ of UK households own a smart speaker (driving "near me" queries)

### Market Size
- UK estate agent industry: GBP 15.2 billion (2026)
- 25,665 active estate agency businesses in the UK (2025)
- Online/hybrid agents handle 5.2% of UK home sales (declining slightly)
- Average UK house price: approx. GBP 300,000 (late 2025)

### Competitive Landscape for Comparison Sites
- **GetAgent** -- Market leader in comparison. Helped 1.2 million+ homeowners. Partners with Which? Powers the TheAdvisory sub-brand. Uses "get" + "agent" -- keyword-rich.
- **EstateAgent4Me** -- Run by HomeOwners Alliance. Covers 18,000+ agents. Descriptive but long/unwieldy domain.
- **CompareAgents** -- Direct descriptor. Hyphenated domain (compare-agents.com) is a known SEO disadvantage.
- **ChooseMyAgent** -- Clear intent but less well-known.
- **OnlineAgentPicker** -- Very descriptive but awkward.
- **BestAgent** -- Superlative claim in the name.

### Domain Extension Patterns (from 300+ proptech analysis)
- 67.4% of proptech companies use exact brand match domains
- .com is overwhelmingly preferred
- .co.uk is standard for UK-only brands
- .io and .ai extensions are growing among tech startups
- Hyphenated domains are advised against (only 2 out of 300+ companies use them)
- Place.com was acquired for $3 million; Knock.com was a strategic investment -- showing the value placed on clean, short brand-match domains

### What Homeowners Value Most (for naming resonance)
- 43% value an agent who can sell at asking price ("best price" / "full value")
- 35% value an agent who can sell quickly ("fast" / "quick")
- 60% prefer to pay on completion (aligns with "no risk" / "free" messaging)

## 6. SUMMARY OF KEY FINDINGS FOR THE NAMING COMMITTEE

1. **The seller journey starts with valuation queries** ("how much is my house worth" at 30,000+/mo). A brand that captures this moment has a strategic advantage.

2. **"Agent" is the most repeated keyword** across high-volume searches but is already heavily used by competitors (GetAgent, ChooseMyAgent, BestAgent, CompareAgents).

3. **"Sell" is the biggest unclaimed keyword** -- despite "sell my house" being 12,000+/mo, no comparison brand incorporates it.

4. **Action verbs perform well** in comparison brand names (Get, Choose, Compare, Find, Pick). They signal intent and match how people search.

5. **Short, single-word brands** (Nested, Settled, Boomin) are the trend among well-funded proptech startups, but they sacrifice SEO for brand memorability.

6. **The "near me" / local trend is accelerating** with voice search and smart speakers. A brand that signals local expertise without being a local agent has a gap to fill.

7. **Kova as a name** is in the "invented/abstract" category (like Zoopla). It has zero inherent SEO keyword value. The committee should weigh whether the brand will have sufficient marketing budget to overcome this, or whether a more keyword-suggestive name would accelerate organic discovery.

8. **"Good agent" is a rising search term** in 2026 specifically, per Estate Agent Today -- indicating a growing consumer desire for quality signals.
