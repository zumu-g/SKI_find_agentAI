## 1. Executive Summary

Kova is an AI-powered estate agent comparison platform that helps UK homeowners find and compare local estate agents using verified performance data. Unlike existing solutions that rely on self-reported statistics or paid placements, Kova leverages HM Land Registry records, verified customer reviews, actual sale times, and asking-price-achieved ratios to deliver transparent, data-driven agent recommendations.

The platform is free for homeowners. Revenue is generated through a referral fee of **0.25% of the final sale price**, capped at **£2,500 + VAT per transaction**, payable by the estate agent only upon successful completion of a sale. This performance-based model aligns incentives across all parties: homeowners get the best agent match, agents receive qualified leads, and Kova earns revenue only when genuine value has been delivered.

**The Opportunity:**

- The UK residential property market processes approximately **1.2 million transactions per year**, with an average sale price of approximately **£290,000**.
- The total addressable market for agent referral fees at Kova's rate is approximately **£870 million per year**.
- Even capturing **1% of UK transactions** by Year 3 would generate annual revenue of **£8.7 million**.
- The estate agent comparison market in the UK is nascent, with no dominant player offering AI-driven, Land-Registry-verified matching.

## 4. Problem & Solution

### 4.1 The Problem

Choosing an estate agent is one of the most consequential financial decisions a UK homeowner makes, yet it is one of the least informed:

**For Homeowners:**

- **No reliable comparison data.** Homeowners typically invite 2-3 local agents for a valuation and choose based on gut feeling, personal rapport, or the highest valuation -- which is often a deliberate overvaluation tactic ("buying the instruction").
- **Information asymmetry.** Agents know their own performance metrics; homeowners do not. There is no accessible, standardised way to compare agents on the metrics that matter: sale-to-asking-price ratio, average time to sell, volume of recent local sales, and customer satisfaction.
- **Pressure sales environment.** The traditional valuation visit is a sales pitch. Homeowners often feel pressured into immediate decisions without adequate data.
- **Costly mistakes.** Choosing the wrong agent can result in a longer time on market, price reductions, fall-throughs, and ultimately tens of thousands of pounds in lost value.

**For Estate Agents:**

- **Expensive lead generation.** Agents spend heavily on Rightmove, Zoopla, portal advertising, leafleting, and canvassing to win instructions. Much of this spend is wasted on unqualified leads.
- **Race to the bottom on fees.** Without a way to demonstrate superior performance, agents compete primarily on fee level, eroding margins.
- **No way to showcase track record.** High-performing agents struggle to differentiate themselves from competitors in a market where consumers cannot easily verify claims.

### 4.2 The Solution

Kova provides a free, AI-powered platform that enables homeowners to compare local estate agents based on verified performance data:

**Core Features:**

1. **Postcode-Based Agent Search.** Homeowners enter their postcode and property details. Kova returns a ranked list of estate agents active in that area.

2. **Verified Performance Data.** Each agent profile displays:
   - Sale-to-asking-price ratio (from Land Registry data cross-referenced with listing data)
   - Average days on market
   - Number of properties sold in the local area (past 12/24 months)
   - Price bracket specialisation
   - Property type specialisation
   - Verified customer reviews and ratings

3. **AI-Powered Matching.** Kova's proprietary matching algorithm considers the homeowner's specific property characteristics (type, estimated value, location, urgency) and matches them with agents who have the strongest verified track record for comparable properties.

4. **Transparent Comparison.** Side-by-side agent comparison on standardised metrics. No paid placements. No agents can buy higher rankings.

5. **Seamless Agent Connection.** Homeowners can request valuations from their preferred agents directly through the platform. Kova manages the introduction and tracks the outcome.

6. **Ongoing Tracking.** Homeowners can track their property's journey from listing through to completion, with milestone updates.

### 4.3 Value Proposition

| Stakeholder | Value Delivered |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Homeowners** | Free access to verified agent data, better agent selection, higher achieved sale prices, faster sales, reduced stress |
| **Estate Agents** | Qualified, high-intent leads; pay only on success; ability to showcase genuine performance; competitive differentiation |
| **The Market** | Greater transparency, improved standards, data-driven decision-making |

## 5. Business Model & Revenue Projections

### 5.1 Revenue Model

Kova operates a **performance-based referral fee model:**

- **Fee:** 0.25% of the final sale price
- **Cap:** £2,500 + VAT per transaction
- **Trigger:** Fee is payable by the estate agent only upon successful completion of the sale (exchange of contracts / completion)
- **No upfront costs** for either homeowners or agents

**Unit Economics:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average UK sale price | £290,000 |
| Referral fee at 0.25% | £725 |
| Fee cap | £2,500 + VAT |
| Cap threshold (sale price) | £1,000,000 |
| Effective fee on £530K (London avg.) | £1,325 |
| Effective fee on £215K (North West avg.) | £537.50 |
| Blended average referral fee (est.) | £725 |

**Agent Perspective:** On a typical £290,000 sale at 1.2% + VAT agent fee, the agent earns £3,480 + VAT. Kova's £725 referral represents approximately **21% of the agent's gross commission** -- competitive with other lead generation costs and significantly lower than many portal and referral fees.

### 5.4 Revenue Recognition

Revenue is recognised at the point of sale completion. Due to the property transaction cycle (typically 12-20 weeks from instruction to completion), there is a natural lag between lead generation activity and revenue recognition. This must be factored into cash flow planning.

**Typical timeline:**
- Month 0: Homeowner visits Kova, compares agents
- Month 0-1: Valuation requests, agent selection
- Month 1-2: Property listed
- Month 2-5: Property on market, viewings, offer accepted
- Month 4-8: Conveyancing, exchange, completion
- Month 8-9: Invoice issued to agent, payment received (30-day terms)

**Implication:** Revenue from early adopters in Month 1 may not be collected until Month 9-12. Working capital must cover this lag.

## 6. Competitive Analysis

### 6.1 Direct Competitors

#### GetAgent (UK)
- **Model:** Agent comparison platform using sold price data
- **Revenue:** Referral fee from agents (estimated 15-20% of agent commission)
- **Strengths:** Established UK brand, Land Registry data integration, coverage across England & Wales
- **Weaknesses:** Higher referral fee for agents, limited AI capability, agent ranking can feel opaque, mixed Trustpilot reviews regarding sales pressure
- **Kova Differentiator:** Lower agent fees (0.25% vs. percentage of commission), AI-powered matching, greater transparency in ranking methodology, no-pressure approach

#### EstateAgent4Me (UK)
- **Model:** Free agent comparison tool
- **Revenue:** Lead generation fees
- **Strengths:** Simple user interface, good local data
- **Weaknesses:** Limited scale, basic comparison metrics, minimal AI, low brand awareness
- **Kova Differentiator:** Deeper data analysis, AI matching, performance-only fee model, superior technology stack

#### LocalAgentFinder (Australia)
- **Model:** Agent comparison platform (Australian market)
- **Revenue:** Referral/lead fees
- **Strengths:** Proven model in Australian market, strong brand recognition
- **Weaknesses:** Not present in UK market, Australian property market has different dynamics
- **Kova Differentiator:** Built specifically for the UK market, UK-specific data sources, HMRC Land Registry integration

### 6.4 Sustainable Competitive Advantages (Moats)

1. **Data Network Effect:** Every transaction processed improves the AI matching model. More data leads to better recommendations, which drives more users, which generates more data.

3. **Trust & Transparency:** First-mover advantage in establishing a genuinely transparent, no-paid-rankings agent comparison brand.
4. **Agent Relationships:** Building a network of participating agents with integrated referral tracking creates switching costs.
5. **AI/ML Models:** Proprietary matching algorithms trained on UK-specific transaction data are difficult to replicate.

### 9.2 Data Architecture

**Core Data Sources:**

1. **HM Land Registry Price Paid Data** -- Free, open data. Updated monthly. Contains: price, date, postcode, property type, new/established, freehold/leasehold. Does not directly identify the selling agent.

2. **Listing Data** -- Sourced from property portals (via partnerships or compliant data acquisition). Contains: listing agent, asking price, listing date, property details. When combined with Land Registry data, enables calculation of sale-to-asking-price ratio and days on market.

3. **Agent Directory Data** -- Companies House, The Property Ombudsman, Propertymark registers. Used to verify agent identity, registration status, and branch locations.

4. **Review Data** -- Collected directly on-platform (verified purchaser reviews), plus aggregation from Google Business, Trustpilot, and AllAgents (subject to terms).

5. **Geospatial Data** -- Ordnance Survey, postcodes.io. Used for catchment area mapping and local market analysis.

**Data Matching Challenge:** Linking Land Registry records (which identify the property and price but not the agent) with listing data (which identifies the agent) is a core technical challenge. Kova's data pipeline uses fuzzy matching on address, price, date, and property attributes to achieve high-confidence agent attribution. This pipeline is a key proprietary asset.

### 9.3 AI/ML Components

1. **Agent Matching Model**
   - Input: Property type, location, estimated value, homeowner preferences (speed vs. price, agent size preference, etc.)
   - Output: Ranked list of agents with match scores
   - Method: Gradient boosted trees (initial), moving to deep learning as data volume grows
   - Training data: Historical transaction outcomes (price achieved, time to sell, customer satisfaction)

2. **Property Valuation Model** (Phase 2)
   - Automated valuation model (AVM) for initial price estimation
   - Helps homeowners set expectations and enables better agent matching

3. **Market Insights Engine**
   - Local market trend analysis
   - Time-to-sell predictions
   - Optimal listing time recommendations

4. **Review Sentiment Analysis**
   - NLP-based analysis of agent reviews
   - Extraction of key themes (communication, negotiation, speed, etc.)

## 12. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### 12.1 Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|----------|------------|
| **Data access restrictions** -- Property portals restrict scraping or data access | High | High | Critical | Build partnerships with data providers; invest in direct data collection; use only openly available Land Registry data as core; explore RESO/API-based data access |
| **Agent resistance** -- Agents refuse to pay referral fees or dispute completions | Medium | High | High | Low fee (0.25%) vs. alternatives; pay-on-success model reduces risk for agents; strong legal agreements; automated completion verification via Land Registry |
| **Slow consumer adoption** -- Homeowners don't change behaviour | Medium | High | High | Invest in SEO/content to capture existing demand; focus on data quality as differentiator; PR strategy to build awareness; offer compelling free tools (valuations) as lead magnets |
| **Competitor response** -- GetAgent or portals launch similar features | Medium | Medium | Medium | Move fast; build data/network moats; focus on transparency as differentiator; patent key AI matching processes |
| **Regulatory change** -- New regulation of estate agent referrals | Low | High | Medium | Proactive engagement with regulators; transparent business model; compliance-first approach; legal counsel on retainer |
| **Data quality issues** -- Land Registry matching errors lead to incorrect agent performance data | Medium | High | High | Rigorous data validation; confidence scoring on matches; clear disclosure of data methodology; manual verification for edge cases |
| **Revenue timing** -- Long lag between lead generation and revenue causes cash flow crisis | High | Medium | High | Conservative cash management; maintain 6+ months runway; consider invoice factoring; negotiate shorter payment terms with agents |
| **Key person dependency** -- Loss of founder(s) or key technical staff | Medium | High | High | Document all processes; build team depth; competitive compensation including equity; advisory board |
| **Market downturn** -- UK property market correction reduces transaction volumes | Medium | Medium | Medium | Transaction volumes are relatively resilient (people still need to move); lower prices reduce average fee but also reduce agent resistance to referral fees; diversify revenue streams |
| **Technology failure** -- Platform outage, data breach, or AI model errors | Low | High | Medium | Cloud-based redundant architecture; comprehensive testing; GDPR-compliant data handling; cyber insurance; incident response plan |

### 12.2 Specific Risk Deep-Dives

#### Data Access Risk (Critical)

The most significant operational risk is the ability to link Land Registry records with listing data to attribute sales to specific agents. Mitigation strategy:

1. **Primary approach:** Land Registry Price Paid Data (open, free) + listing data from property portals
2. **Fallback 1:** Direct data partnerships with property data aggregators (e.g., Hometrack, TwentyCI, Dataloft)
3. **Fallback 2:** Agent self-reported data with verification against Land Registry
4. **Fallback 3:** Freedom of Information requests to Land Registry for additional data fields
5. **Long-term:** Build proprietary data collection through platform usage and agent partnerships

#### Agent Fee Collection Risk

Agents may dispute referral fees or delay payment. Mitigation:

1. Clear, signed referral agreements before any leads are sent
2. Automated completion monitoring via Land Registry
3. Transparent invoicing with full audit trail
4. Legal recourse clauses in agreements
5. Blacklisting of non-paying agents (removal from platform)
6. Consider GoCardless Direct Debit mandates for smoother collection
